Track #5 of the [[study-plan-gaps|study plan]]. Fluent technical communication in
English — the point about "frequent technical discussions in English with the
client".

## Goal

It's not perfect grammar — it's **explaining architecture, trade-offs and
decisions** clearly and confidently, and **leading** technical discussions.

## Practice areas

- **Think out loud**: narrate your reasoning while solving a problem.
- **STAR** for behavioral questions (Situation, Task, Action, Result).
- **Explain trade-offs**: "We chose X over Y because…", "The downside is…".
- **Clarifying questions**: "Just to confirm, do you mean…?",
  "What are the constraints around…?".
- **Disagree politely**: "I see your point, however…",
  "An alternative would be…".
- **Buy time**: "Let me think through this for a second."

## System design / fullstack vocabulary

- scalability, throughput, latency, bottleneck, trade-off, eventual consistency,
  idempotency, backpressure, race condition, cache invalidation, observability.

## Suggested routine

- Explain **each note in this garden** out loud in English.
- Mock interviews (Pramp / interviewing.io / with peers).
- Record yourself answering questions and review.
- Shadowing tech talks (repeat along for rhythm/pronunciation).
- Daily standup in English (even alone, simulate it).

## Anchor phrases (drill until automatic)

- "Let me walk you through my approach."
- "There are a couple of ways to tackle this."
- "In my previous role at Seguralta, I…"
- "To summarize, the key trade-off here is…"
